# Executive Summary: Operational Efficiency Analysis
## Prepared by: Bhavana Gurajala | Date: January 2026

---

## Purpose
This report summarizes findings from a comprehensive process improvement 
analysis conducted across key business units. The goal was to identify 
inefficiencies and provide actionable recommendations to improve 
operational efficiency by 30%+.

---

## Key Findings

| Finding | Impact | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Manual reporting causing 3-5 day delays | High | Critical |
| No centralized requirements tracking | Medium | High |
| Inconsistent sprint planning across teams | Medium | High |
| Missing data validation on ETL pipelines | High | Critical |
| No formal stakeholder sign-off process | Low | Medium |

---

## Recommendations
1. Automate reporting through Power BI connected to live SQL data
2. Centralize all requirements in JIRA with defined workflows
3. Standardize Agile sprint cycles across all teams
4. Implement SQL validation scripts on every data pipeline refresh
5. Introduce formal stakeholder sign-off documentation

---

## Projected Outcomes
- 30%+ improvement in operational efficiency within 2 quarters
- Reporting delivery time reduced from 5 days to same-day
- 100% stakeholder visibility into project status at all times

---

## Conclusion
Implementing the above recommendations will significantly reduce bottlenecks,
improve cross-team alignment, and enable faster, more informed business decisions.
